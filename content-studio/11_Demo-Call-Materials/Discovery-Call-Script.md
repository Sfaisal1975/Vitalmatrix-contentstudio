# Discovery Call Script — Dr Faisal Guide
**Section:** 11_Demo-Call-Materials
**Status:** DRAFT — W08 + Dr Faisal review required
**Owner:** W05 + Dr Faisal
**Format:** Internal reference — not shared with practitioner
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Audience tier:** INTERNAL (D-57)

---

## Call Structure

| Minutes | Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 0–2 | Warm open | Establish peer rapport, not sales dynamic |
| 2–8 | Discovery — their practice | Understand clinical model before presenting anything |
| 8–18 | Platform overview | Tailored to what they just told you |
| 18–24 | Objection handling / questions | Let them lead |
| 24–28 | Next step | Clear, one ask |
| 28–30 | Close | Warm, no pressure |

---

## Opening (0–2 min)

"Thanks for making time — I know 30 minutes in a clinical schedule is not nothing. Before I tell you anything about VitalMatrix, I'd like to understand how you work, if that's alright. I find the conversation is much more useful that way."

*[Do not pitch anything yet. Ask first.]*

---

## Discovery Questions (2–8 min)

Choose 3–4 from this list based on what they mentioned in their enquiry:

**On clinical workflow:**
- "When you're building a clinical picture for a complex patient — multi-system, multi-terrain — what does your documentation process look like?"
- "How long does it take you to produce a working clinical framework for that kind of case?"
- "Are you currently using the IFM matrix format, or have you developed your own approach over time?"

**On pain points:**
- "What's the part of the clinical documentation process that takes the most time?"
- "Do you find yourself repeating the same structural thinking across similar case types?"
- "Is there a gap between the quality of your clinical reasoning and the speed at which you can document it?"

**On practice context:**
- "What does your patient population look like — complex chronic, primarily one terrain type, or broad mix?"
- "Are you working solo or with associates who also produce clinical documentation?"

*[Listen carefully. Use their language — not VitalMatrix language — when you reflect back.]*

---

## Platform Overview (8–18 min)

**Frame it to their answers.** Do not deliver a generic pitch. Use the structure below and adapt:

**Opening frame:**
"Based on what you've described — [their specific situation] — let me show you exactly where VitalMatrix fits into that."

**Core explanation (always include):**
"VitalMatrix takes the signals you have at the end of a consultation — symptoms, lab results, terrain history — and maps them across a seven-node terrain architecture. It identifies which nodes carry primary burden, how the zones interact, where cascade propagation is active, and it produces a structured working document: the terrain architecture. The thing you used to spend [X time — use their number] building by hand."

**Adapt based on their profile:**

*If they mentioned time pressure:*
"The generation is under 60 seconds. The document is a starting point for your review — you annotate, remove, adjust. But the skeleton is there."

*If they mentioned clinical complexity / IFM alignment:*
"The architecture is aligned with IFM terrain principles. It is not a general health app or a diagnostic tool. It was designed by a functional medicine clinician for functional medicine practice — which is a different design brief from anything else in this space."

*If they mentioned practitioner burnout / documentation burden:*
"The terrain architecture used to be the most demanding cognitive output of the consultation. VitalMatrix takes the structural computation off your plate without removing your clinical judgment from the process. The decision is always yours."

**Founding cohort (always include, state simply):**
"Phase 1 is limited to 10 founding practitioners. You would be one of ten. That matters because founding practitioners shape the terrain engine — your case patterns and feedback feed directly into how the platform develops. The rate is £299 a month with a 60-month guarantee. It goes up at Phase 2."

## Common Objections (18–24 min)

**"I'm not sure I have time to learn a new system."**
"That's a fair concern. Orientation is 20 minutes. The first case takes longer — maybe 10 minutes of data entry. After three or four cases, most practitioners report it's faster than their previous process. But I'd rather you try it and tell me that's not true than take my word for it."

**"How is this different from ChatGPT or other AI tools?"**
"It's a different design problem. General AI tools are trained on general medical literature. VitalMatrix is built on a proprietary terrain architecture — the NCZ framework — that reflects how functional medicine actually works: node burden, zone interaction, cascade propagation. General tools don't have that structure. They'll give you a list. VitalMatrix gives you a clinical architecture."

**"I'm worried about patient data and GDPR."**
"The platform does not store patient-identifiable information. You enter clinical signals — symptoms, lab values, terrain flags. No names, no NHS numbers, nothing identifiable. The output document is generated and returned to you. We are ICO registered (ZC101813) and have taken legal advice on our data position."

**"£299 a month feels significant for a new platform."**
"I understand that. It is a commitment. The question is whether the time it saves justifies the cost — and that depends on your hourly rate and how many complex cases you see per week. For most functional medicine practitioners I've spoken to, recovering two to three hours of documentation time per week more than covers it. But I'd encourage you to make that calculation for your own practice."

**"Can I try it before committing?"**
"There is no free trial in Phase 1 — the founding cohort model doesn't work with a trial structure because of how calibration works. What I can offer is full transparency on the first 30 days: if after 30 days of genuine use it is not working for your practice, contact me directly and we will discuss it. I am not interested in a founding practitioner who isn't getting value."

**"I need to think about it / discuss with a partner."**
"Of course. Can I ask — is there a specific question or concern that would help that conversation? Sometimes I can give you something concrete to take into it."

## Next Step Ask (24–28 min)

**If they seem ready:**
"The next step is straightforward — I send you an onboarding confirmation, your credentials arrive within 24 hours, and we schedule a 20-minute orientation session. Do you want to proceed?"

**If they need more time:**
"That's fine. I'll send you the founding practitioner summary after this call — it covers everything we've discussed. If you're ready to move forward, reply to that email and I'll initiate onboarding. The Phase 1 window closes on [DATE], so you have time, but not unlimited time."

**If they're clearly not a fit:**
"Based on what you've described, I want to be honest — I'm not sure the timing is right for your practice at this stage. [Specific reason]. I'd rather tell you that now than have you join the founding cohort and not get value from it. Let's stay in touch for Phase 2."

---

## Close (28–30 min)

"Thank you for the time. I'll send a follow-up email within the hour — it will summarise what we covered and have a clear next step. Is there anything else before I let you go?"

*[Do not extend the call. End on time. Send the follow-up within 60 minutes.]*
