## PP-04 — Treatment Resistance (TerrainLock)

**Hook type:** TYPE 3 (Counterintuitive)
**Target length:** 160–200 words

---

**Subject:** Your patient is following the protocol. Nothing is improving. Here is why.

**Preview text:** Treatment resistance is not always a protocol problem. Sometimes it is a cascade problem.

---

The most difficult patients in functional medicine practice are not the ones with the most severe presentations.

They are the ones who do the work — who follow the nutritional protocol, take the supplements, reduce the stressors — and do not improve.

The clinical instinct is to modify the protocol. Add a layer. Change the sequencing. Try a different approach to the same problem.

The correct clinical instinct is to check the cascade.

TerrainLock — the Z2-Z1-Z5-Z2 self-perpetuating loop — is the pattern that most commonly explains treatment resistance in complex functional medicine cases. When Z2 burden drives Z1 burden, which drives Z5 burden, which feeds back into Z2, treating any single zone in isolation is insufficient. The loop reloads.

VitalMatrix detects TerrainLock automatically and distinguishes it from standard multi-zone burden. The clinical response changes: clear the driver first. Stop modifying the downstream protocol.

Some patients do not improve despite correct protocols because the protocol is treating the wrong zone.
