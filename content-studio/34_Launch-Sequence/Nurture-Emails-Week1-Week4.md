A practitioner identifies active hormonal burden. They address Z5. The patient improves temporarily. Six weeks later, the same presentation returns — sometimes worse. The reason is almost always the same: the upstream source was never cleared.

S1 is the most common cascade pattern in the VitalMatrix clinical database. From Z1, burden flows into Z5 via a second cascade. The hormonal presentation is real. The gut was always the source.

Treating downstream without clearing upstream is not poor clinical judgement. Without cascade mapping, it is the only option available.
