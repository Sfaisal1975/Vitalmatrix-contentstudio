## WEEK 5 — TerrainLock

**Hook type:** TYPE 3 (Counterintuitive)
**Target length:** 180–220 words

---

**Subject:** Z2 drives Z1 drives Z5 drives Z2. Your patient is stuck.

**Preview text:** TerrainLock explains why some patients do not improve despite correct protocols.

---

There is a specific pattern in complex functional medicine patients that no single-zone intervention will resolve.

Z2 — the gastrointestinal terrain — drives burden into Z1, the metabolic-energetic terrain. Z1 then drives burden into Z5, the hormonal terrain axis. And Z5 — through inflammatory and hormonal signalling — drives burden back into Z2.

The loop is self-perpetuating. When all three cascade stacks are active simultaneously, treating any one zone in isolation is insufficient. The upstream source keeps reloading.

VitalMatrix calls this TerrainLock. The platform detects it automatically, flags it explicitly, and distinguishes it from standard multi-zone burden. The clinical implication is significant: TerrainLock cases require the Driver zone to be cleared first. Not the zone with the worst symptoms. The zone that is sustaining the loop.

Z2 is the default Driver in most TerrainLock presentations. The gut is not always symptomatic. It is often silent. It is almost always causal.

The practitioners who recognise this pattern do not chase symptoms. They identify the loop, clear the driver, and watch the other zones resolve downstream.

## WEEK 6 — The DRD Designation

**Hook type:** TYPE 1 (Clinical Observation)
**Target length:** 160–200 words

---

**Subject:** Which zone do you treat first? The answer is not obvious.

**Preview text:** The highest burden zone is not always the Driver. Here is how VitalMatrix decides.

---

In multi-zone presentations, the instinct is to treat the zone with the highest composite score first.

This is not always correct.

The DRD — Driver-Reactor Designation — ranks zones by burden and identifies which carries the highest clinical load. But the computation does more than rank. It traces the cascade logic to identify which zone, if cleared, would produce the most benefit across all active zones. The Driver zone is the one whose burden is sustaining the others.

VitalMatrix shows every step of this reasoning. The computation trace is not hidden behind a recommendation. It is visible, verifiable, and reviewable by the practitioner before any clinical decision is made.

When two zones score identically, Z2 is the default tiebreaker. That is a documented clinical decision, not an algorithmic convenience. The reasoning is embedded in the platform and disclosed in every output.

You are not asked to trust the result. You are shown how it was reached.
