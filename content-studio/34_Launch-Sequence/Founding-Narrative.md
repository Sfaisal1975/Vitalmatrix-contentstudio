## The Origin Story

### Why VitalMatrix exists — in Dr Faisal's words

For 26 years, I have practised functional medicine. In consultation after consultation, I built IFM matrices by hand. I mapped terrain signals across biological nodes, identified cascade interactions, weighted burden scores, and generated clinical documentation — all from scratch, for every patient, in every session.

The work was clinically sound. The process was not sustainable.

Functional medicine demands a level of systems thinking that conventional clinical software was never designed to support. The tools built for general practice — EMRs, clinical management systems — were designed for a diagnostic model that does not reflect terrain medicine. They record. They do not compute terrain relationships.

So I built what should have existed.

VitalMatrix is the infrastructure I needed and did not have. It maps presenting symptoms and lab signals across the NCZ™ terrain architecture — 7 biological nodes, 5 terrain zones, 5,000+ computation algorithms — and generates the working clinical document I used to spend hours producing.

The document is a starting point, not a conclusion. The clinical decision remains with the practitioner. That is by design, and by regulatory requirement.

## The Founding Cohort Rationale

Phase 1 of VitalMatrix is limited to 10 founding practitioners. This is a deliberate architectural decision, not a marketing tactic.

The terrain engine learns. The first 10 practices provide the calibration data — the clinical patterns, edge cases, and feedback signals — that the platform's 12 learning channels use to refine output quality. The founding cohort does not just use the platform. They shape it.

This is why founding practitioners receive:
- Direct access to Dr Faisal's team for clinical feedback on platform outputs
- Input into feature prioritisation and terrain architecture development
- A 60-month pricing guarantee at the Phase 1 rate (£299/month)
- Permanent Founding Practitioner designation in the cohort record

At Phase 2, the rate increases. The founding cohort closes. There are no retrospective admissions.
