# DOCUMENT 1 — EMAIL HOOKS LIBRARY

The hook is the first 1–2 sentences of the email body. It determines whether the email gets read past line 3.

For functional medicine practitioners — a highly educated, time-pressed, scepticism-trained audience — the hook must do one of three things instantly:
1. **Name a clinical reality they live** — they nod and keep reading
2. **Say something they have not seen said** — they are curious enough to continue
3. **Signal peer-to-peer respect** — they do not feel sold to

What a hook must never do: open with a compliment, a question about their wellbeing, a generic health claim, or anything that signals "marketing email." These practitioners delete that category in under two seconds.

## The Six Hook Types

### TYPE 1 — Clinical Observation
Opens with a specific, accurate clinical observation from practice.
**Best for:** N1 nurture, thought leadership, post-call Version A
**Formula:** State a specific clinical truth without editorialising.

Examples:
- "In functional medicine, the most diagnostically complex patients are rarely complex because of a single node. They are complex because of what is happening between nodes."
- "The documentation bottleneck in terrain-based practice is not writing the plan. It is producing the architecture that the plan depends on."
- "Every IFM-trained practitioner I have spoken to describes the same experience: the clinical reasoning is there before the consultation ends. The documentation takes hours after."
- "The gap between what a functional medicine practitioner knows in the room and what they can document before the next patient is the single biggest bottleneck in the field."

Never: "Functional medicine is changing healthcare as we know it." / "As a fellow clinician, I know how busy your practice is."

---

### TYPE 2 — Direct Situation
Opens by describing the practitioner's working day with enough specificity it feels observed.
**Best for:** N2 nurture, pre-call email, onboarding emails
**Formula:** Describe a specific moment in their working day without naming them.

Examples:
- "You have just finished a consultation with a patient presenting across four or five systems. You know what the terrain picture is. Writing it down will take another hour."
- "There is a specific moment in a complex case when the clinical picture is clear and the documentation has not started. VitalMatrix exists for that moment."
- "If you are currently building IFM matrices by hand, this email is relevant to you. If you are not, it probably is not."
- "Most of the practitioners I speak to describe the same constraint: the clinical thinking is not the problem. The time to document it properly is."

Never: "I hope this email finds you well." / "You are probably very busy, so I will keep this short."

---

### TYPE 3 — Counterintuitive
Opens with something that contradicts the expected framing.
**Best for:** N3 nurture, LinkedIn posts, conference materials
**Formula:** State the opposite of what they expect, then resolve it.

Examples:
- "VitalMatrix is not for every functional medicine practitioner. Most of the calls I take end with me saying the timing is not right."
- "The founding cohort is 10 practitioners. That is not an early-access limitation. It is the number the architecture requires."
- "The most common question I get is whether VitalMatrix replaces clinical judgement. The answer is no — and it should not. A platform that removed the clinician from the decision would be clinically dangerous and commercially worthless."
- "Functional medicine does not have a thinking problem. It has an infrastructure problem."

---

### TYPE 4 — Personal Story
Dr Faisal's own clinical experience as the opening. Highest trust-building type. Requires real, specific stories.
**Best for:** N1 nurture (with Dr Faisal's context), founding narrative emails

Examples (placeholders — replace with Dr Faisal's actual stories):
- "I had a patient who had seen eleven practitioners before she reached me. The notes from each one described different presentations. None of them described the same terrain."
- "For 26 years I built these matrices by hand. For 26 years the clinical reasoning was faster than the documentation. I assumed that was just how it was."
- "There was a point in my practice where I was spending more time on the architecture of a case than on the conversation with the patient. That is when I knew the infrastructure had to change."

---

### TYPE 5 — Factual Anchor
Opens with one specific number or fact, stated without commentary.
**Best for:** N2 nurture, website homepage, investor materials
**Formula:** One fact that forces the reader to contextualise it.

Examples:
- "26 years of functional medicine practice. For most of that time, I built IFM matrices by hand."
- "5,000+ terrain computation algorithms. One clinical document. Under 60 seconds."
- "Ten founding practitioner slots. Not eleven. Not twenty. Ten."
- "The NCZ™ framework maps seven biological nodes across five terrain zones. It was not designed in a lab. It was developed over two decades of clinical practice."
- "VitalMatrix Ltd. Company No. 17046123. ICO ZC101813. Founded by a practitioner, not a software company."

Never: unverifiable statistics / superlatives ("the only", "the most advanced") / rounded numbers ("100s of algorithms")

---

### TYPE 6 — Soft Close
Opens by acknowledging position without pressure. Only for final-stage emails.
**Best for:** N4 nurture, PATH B follow-ups, launch close email
**Critical rule:** Never use before N4/final stage. Used too early it reads as resignation. Used at the right moment it reads as respect.

Examples:
- "I know how busy clinical practice is. I have been in it for 26 years."
- "If now is not the right time, that is completely fine. I would rather you reach out when the moment is right than feel any pressure from this email."
- "This is a short note. Phase 1 closes on [DATE]. After that, the founding cohort is complete."
- "No follow-up after this one. If it is right for your practice, the link below goes straight to my calendar."

# DOCUMENT 2 — EMAIL STYLE GUIDE

## Two Email Registers

| Register | When used | Visual style | Tone |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------|
| **Brand Email** | Nurture sequence, launch sequence, notifications | Dark navy template with gold accent | Professional, peer-to-peer, branded |
| **Personal Email** | Post-call follow-ups, check-ins, founder updates | Plain text or minimal formatting | Direct, personal, Dr Faisal's voice |

Critical: Never use the dark navy template for a post-call follow-up. It destroys the personal register.

## Brand Email — Colour Values (copy exactly)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Outer background | #f4f4f2 |
| Email body background | #0D2B4E |
| Brand mark text | #C9A84C |
| H2 headline | #F4F4F2 |
| Body text | rgba(244,244,242,0.82) |
| CTA button background | #C9A84C |
| CTA button text | #0D2B4E |

## Brand Email — Typography

| Element | Size | Weight |
|---------|------|--------|
| Brand mark | 11px | 700 — Monospace, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.18em |
| H2 headline | 22px | 400 |
| Body text | 15px | 400 — line-height 1.7 |
| CTA button | 11px | 700 — Monospace, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.12em |

## CTA Button Rules
- Always UPPERCASE
- Always end with → (right arrow)
- Maximum 5 words
- Standard: BOOK THE DISCOVERY CALL → / READ MORE → / REPLY TO THIS EMAIL →

## Mandatory Footer (always included on brand emails)
> VitalMatrix Ltd | Company No. 17046123 | ICO ZC101813
> Clinical intelligence platform for functional medicine practitioners.
> VitalMatrix™ outputs are terrain support considerations only — not diagnoses.

## Personal Email Rules
1. No brand template — plain text only
2. No CTA button — hyperlinked text or bare URL
3. No header brand mark
4. Maximum 3–4 sentences per paragraph (often 1–2)
5. No bullet lists — use sentences
6. Minimal signature: Dr Shahzad Faisal / MBBS, FAAMFM | Founder, VitalMatrix™

## Length Guidelines

| Email | Target | Max |
|-------|--------|-----|
| N1 Nurture Day 2 | 180–220 words | 280 |
| N2 Nurture Day 4 | 160–200 words | 250 |
| N3 Nurture Day 6 | 150–180 words | 220 |
| N4 Nurture Day 8 | 100–130 words | 160 |
| Pre-call | 80–100 words | 130 |
| Post-call Version A | 120–150 words | 180 |
| Post-call Version B | 180–220 words | 260 |
| Post-call Version C | 80–100 words | 130 |
| Welcome email | 150–180 words | 220 |
| Week 1 check-in | 100–120 words | 150 |
| Monthly founder update | 250–350 words | 450 |

General rule: the later in the funnel, the shorter the email.

## What Never Appears in a VitalMatrix Email
- Stock photo header images
- Social media icon footer bars
- Countdown timers
- Progress bars / gamification
- GIFs or animated elements
- Exclamation marks (zero is often better than one)
- "Just checking in"
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- Red or green text

# DOCUMENT 4 — PHASE 1 PRACTITIONER ACQUISITION PLAYBOOK

## The 7-Stage Funnel

STAGE 1 — ENQUIRY RECEIVED → STAGE 2 — NURTURE SEQUENCE (Days 2–8, automated) → STAGE 3 — DISCOVERY CALL BOOKED → STAGE 4 — DISCOVERY CALL COMPLETED → STAGE 5 — DECISION → STAGE 6 — ONBOARDING (Days 0–7) → STAGE 7 — ACTIVATED FOUNDING PRACTITIONER

## Stage 1 — Enquiry Received
System fires automatically: confirmation email to practitioner + notification to hello@vitalmatrix.co.uk + 4-email nurture sequence queued.
Team actions (manual, same day): log in lead tracker, assess ICP score, personal email from Dr Faisal within 24h if HOT lead.

## Stage 2 — Nurture Sequence (automated)

| Email | Day | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|
| N1 | Day 2 | Credibility — 26 years, Harley Street, why he built this |
| N2 | Day 4 | Clinical use case — NCZ™, terrain architecture |
| N3 | Day 6 | Exclusivity — £299/mo, 60-month guarantee, founding designation |
| N4 | Day 8 | Soft final nudge — direct to Calendly |

## Stage 3 — Discovery Call Booked
Pre-call email sent 24h before. Dr Faisal reviews lead notes 15 minutes before. Pre-call prep: pull original enquiry, note ICP score, note which email triggered booking, have call script open.

No-show protocol: wait 5 minutes → brief warm email → if no response in 48h → one final outreach → COLD status.

## Stage 4 — Call Completed
Post-call email within 60 minutes (non-negotiable). Log outcome. Assess path.

## Stage 5 — Decision: Three Paths

**PATH A — Proceeding:** Welcome email within 2h → credentials within 4h → orientation session within 24h → update founding slots counter.

**PATH B — Needs time:** Version B email within 60min → follow-up Day 4 → follow-up Day 8 → Phase 1 close date email. Never follow up more than twice after the call. Third chase is pressure, not persistence.

**PATH C — Not a fit:** Version C within 60min → log reason → set Phase 2 reminder. Every not-a-fit today is a Phase 2 prospect. Leave them warm.

## Stage 6 — Onboarding (Days 0–7)
Day 0: Welcome email + credentials. Days 1–2: Orientation session (20 min video call). Day 7: Personal check-in from Dr Faisal. Day 14 if no response: Dr Faisal phones directly.

## ICP Qualification Criteria

**HOT lead signals** (personal outreach within 24h):
- Mentions specific IFM terminology (node, terrain, cascade, FLINT, COMPASS)
- Currently building matrices by hand, mentions time cost
- Senior practitioner (10+ years, known in IFM community)
- Reached out after a LinkedIn post
- Referred by another practitioner

**WARM lead:** mentions functional medicine but not specific methodology. Standard nurture.

**COLD lead:** general health enquiry, no FM context, patient or patient-adjacent. Nurture only, no personal outreach.

## ICP Minimum Criteria

| Criteria | Ideal | Disqualifying |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| IFM training | IFM certified (FAAMFM, IFMCP) | No functional medicine exposure |
| Case complexity | 5+ complex multi-system cases/week | Primarily simple/acute presentations |
| Practice type | Private functional medicine | NHS-only |
| Patient volume | 15+ patients/week | < 8 patients/week |
| Geography | UK-based | Outside UK |

## Conversion Targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Enquiry → call booked | 25–35% |
| Call booked → call completed | 75%+ |
| Call completed → PATH A | 40–60% |
| Onboarded → activated | 90%+ |
| Activated → Month 1 renewal | 95%+ |

Founding cohort target: 10 activated practitioners. Revenue at full cohort: £35,880/year.

## Failure Recovery Protocols

- **FR-01 Bounced email:** Check Resend dashboard. Hard bounce → Dr Faisal personal email from own inbox.
- **FR-02 No pre-call email sent:** Send immediately, even same-day.
- **FR-03 Call overruns:** Let it run. It's a buying signal.
- **FR-04 Competitor mentioned:** "That's worth knowing — what attracted you to them?" Never disparage.
- **FR-05 Free trial requested:** Use objection script. No improvised trial. Founding model depends on it.
- **FR-06 Phase 1 closes with fewer than 10:** Maximum 2-week extension, communicated once to PENDING only. Never announce publicly.
- **FR-07 Founding practitioner goes silent post-onboarding:** Week 1 check-in → Day 14 phone call → 30-day health check call.

## The 10 Rules of Phase 1

1. Qualify before you nurture. HOT/WARM/COLD at Stage 1.
2. Never pitch before you discover. Open with questions, not demo.
3. Send post-call email within 60 minutes.
4. Choose correct post-call version (A/B/C).
5. Never offer a free trial.
6. The founding cohort is deliberate, not desperate.
7. Log everything. Phase 2 is built on Phase 1 data.
8. Dr Faisal's personal involvement is the single biggest conversion asset. It is the strategy, not an optional escalation.
9. Preserve Phase 2 relationships.
10. The close date is real.
