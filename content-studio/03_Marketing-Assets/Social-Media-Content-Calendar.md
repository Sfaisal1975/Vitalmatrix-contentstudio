## Platform Strategy Overview

Each platform serves a different role in the VitalMatrix funnel. Content is not simply cross-posted — it is adapted per platform.

| Platform | Primary audience | Role in funnel | Tone |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|------|
| **LinkedIn** | Functional medicine practitioners, IFM-trained clinicians, integrative GPs | Practitioner acquisition — primary channel | Peer-to-peer, clinical, professional |
| **Facebook** | Patient/consumer audience, practitioner communities, health-conscious professionals | Patient awareness + practitioner community | Slightly warmer, educational, accessible |
| **Instagram** | Visual health content consumers, patient-facing, brand building | Brand trust + visual authority | Visual-first, elegant, authoritative |
| **X (Twitter)** | Clinical opinion leaders, health journalists, policy commentators, MHRA-adjacent | Thought leadership + crisis response (MHRA) | Sharp, brief, factual, occasionally bold |

## Content Repurposing Workflow

Every piece of long-form content produced for VitalMatrix must be broken into platform-native formats before publishing. This workflow prevents the team from starting from scratch each week.

```
Source content
     │
     ├── LinkedIn article (500–1200 words)
     │        │
     │        ├── LinkedIn post (250 words — hook + key insight + CTA)
     │        ├── Facebook post (150 words — simplified, patient-accessible)
     │        ├── Instagram caption (100 words + image card)
     │        └── 2× X threads or standalone tweets
     │
     ├── Nurture email (N1–N4)
     │        │
     │        ├── LinkedIn post (Day 3 after email send — same theme, different angle)
     │        └── Instagram quote card (key line from the email)
     │
     └── Discovery call talking points
              │
              └── LinkedIn post (anonymised clinical scenario — "I spoke to a practitioner recently who...")
```

**Repurposing rule:** Never copy-paste between platforms. Always rewrite for the platform register. The ideas travel, the words do not.

## Post Templates by Platform

### LinkedIn Post Template

```
[HOOK — 1–2 sentences. No "I'm excited to share." No emojis in opening line.]

[BODY — 3–4 short paragraphs. Each paragraph 1–3 sentences. 
Leave a blank line between each. 
Clinical, specific, peer-to-peer.]

[CLOSING LINE — one sentence. Either a question that invites comments, 
or a statement that earns a reaction.]

[CTA — optional, used in Post 2/Post 3 only]
→ Book a discovery call: [link]
→ 10 founding slots. [N] remaining.

[HASHTAGS — 3–5 maximum. Never in the body — always at the end.]
#FunctionalMedicine #IntegrativeMedicine #VitalMatrix
```

**LinkedIn rules:**
- Never open with a question (algorithmic penalty + practitioner delete reflex)
- Never use emojis in the first two lines
- Use 3–5 hashtags maximum — never more
- The first 2 lines appear before "see more" — make them count
- Tag Dr Faisal's profile on all posts published from the VitalMatrix page

---

### Facebook Post Template

```
[HOOK — slightly warmer than LinkedIn. One question or one observation is acceptable.]

[BODY — 2–3 paragraphs. More conversational than LinkedIn. 
Educational or personal-story framing preferred.
Max 200 words total.]

[CTA if applicable]

[HASHTAGS — 2–3 maximum on Facebook]
```

**Facebook rules:**
- Facebook organic reach favours posts that generate comments — end with a genuine question
- Do not share bare links as the primary post — embed link in comment or use post body only
- Videos outperform images on Facebook — if Multimedia Library (Section 12) has short clips, use them

---

### Instagram Caption Template

```
[HOOK LINE — 1 sentence. Appears before "more". Must be compelling standalone.]

[BODY — 3–5 short sentences or a 3-line list. 
Warm but authoritative.]

[CTA]
Link in bio → [destination]

[HASHTAGS — 10–20, placed after a line break]

#FunctionalMedicine #IntegrativeMedicine #TerrainMedicine 
#ChronicIllness #FunctionalMedicineDoctor #VitalMatrix
#HolisticHealth #RootCause #GutHealth #HormoneHealth
#UKHealth #LondonClinic #HarleyStreet
```

**Instagram rules:**
- The image carries the post — caption amplifies it, not the other way around
- All brand images follow the gold-on-navy or navy-on-cream palette (see `06_Brand-Assets/Email-Style-Guide.md`)
- Stories can be more casual and ephemeral — do not apply the same rigour as feed posts
- Reels (short video) have 3× the organic reach of static images — prioritise when video content exists

---

### X (Twitter) Post Template

```
[1 sentence — factual, sharp, no filler. 
Clinical observation or platform fact.
Maximum 200 characters if possible — leaves room for RT commentary.]

[Optional 2nd tweet if threading:]
[Elaboration or example — still short. No rambling.]

[Link if applicable — counts toward character limit]

[Hashtags — 1–2 maximum on X. Often zero.]
```

**X rules:**
- X has the most aggressive delete reflex of any platform for this audience — be direct
- Clinical opinion pieces perform better than promotional posts
- Do not post more than once per day (looks like spam to followers)
- If responding to MHRA news, regulatory changes, or clinical research — act within 2 hours (see Section 29 Crisis Playbook)
- No threads longer than 4 tweets for VitalMatrix content

## Scheduling Workflow (End-to-End)

```
Monday morning (weekly planning session — 30 minutes)
   │
   ├── 1. Review previous week's post performance
   ├── 2. Select themes from monthly calendar above
   ├── 3. Draft all posts for the week (LinkedIn × 3, Facebook × 2, Instagram × 3, X × 4)
   ├── 4. W08 review — check against compliance checklist below
   ├── 5. Submit via API admin endpoint POST /social/schedule (see Social-Automation-Setup.md)

```

## Compliance Checklist (pre-publishing, every post)

- [ ] No unregistered clinical claims (cross-reference `21_Evidence-Register/Claims-Register.md`)
- [ ] No guaranteed outcome language ("will improve", "cures", "eliminates")
- [ ] No patient data or identifiable case details
- [ ] Hashtags within platform limits
- [ ] Image uses correct brand palette (gold #C9A84C / navy #0D2B4E / cream #F4F4F2)
- [ ] LinkedIn posts: first 2 lines work as standalone hook
- [ ] X posts: no threads longer than 4 tweets
- [ ] MHRA-adjacent claims reviewed against Section 18 (Regulatory Compliance)
- [ ] W08 approved before submission to scheduler
