## 1. What VitalMatrix produces

For every case you enter, VitalMatrix generates a **Working Terrain Architecture** document. This document contains:

- **NCZ™ Node Burden Map** — identifies which of the 7 biological nodes carry primary terrain burden based on the signals you enter
- **Terrain Zone Interaction Summary** — how burden is distributed across the 5 terrain zones
- **FLINT™ Score** — a single composite score reflecting overall terrain load (used for tracking change over time, not for diagnosis)
- **Cascade Pathway Indicator** — identifies active cascade propagation between nodes
- **Protocol Scaffold** — a structured list of terrain support considerations aligned to IFM principles, for your clinical review

The document is a starting point. All clinical decisions remain with you.

## 3. Entering a case

**Step 1 — Presenting signals**
Enter the patient's primary presenting symptoms. Use clinical language — the terrain engine maps symptom clusters to node burden distributions. Be specific: duration, severity pattern, and aggravating/relieving factors improve output accuracy.

**Step 2 — Lab data (optional but recommended)**
Enter available lab results. The platform accepts functional reference ranges where standard ranges are not applicable — you can flag this in the input field.

**Step 3 — Terrain history flags**
Mark any known terrain history: prior diagnoses, significant stressors, medication burden, dietary pattern, sleep quality. Each flag adjusts node weighting.

**Step 4 — Generate**
The platform processes the inputs and generates the Working Terrain Architecture document. This typically takes under 60 seconds.

---

## 4. Using the output in consultation

The Working Terrain Architecture is designed to be used during or immediately after the consultation — not instead of it. Suggested workflow:

1. Review the NCZ™ Node Burden Map — confirm or challenge the node distribution against your clinical assessment
2. Check the Cascade Pathway Indicator — cascade activity often reveals the priority sequencing for intervention
3. Use the Protocol Scaffold as your starting structure — annotate, remove, or add to it based on your clinical judgement
4. Save or export the document for the patient record

**If the output does not reflect your clinical reasoning:** This is feedback the platform needs. Email hello@vitalmatrix.co.uk with the case summary (no patient-identifiable information) and your clinical assessment. This is how the terrain engine improves.

---

## 5. What the platform does not do

- It does not diagnose
- It does not prescribe
- It does not replace IFM clinical training or clinical judgement
- It does not access patient records or connect to any external clinical system
- It does not retain patient data beyond your session unless you explicitly save

---

## 6. Regulatory position

VitalMatrix operates as a clinical intelligence platform. It is not a medical device under MHRA classification (consulted position — see `18_Regulatory-Compliance/` for full regulatory position documentation).

All content generated by the platform is for practitioner use only. It must not be shared directly with patients as a clinical report or treatment plan.
