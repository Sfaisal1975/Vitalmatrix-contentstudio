## Your First Patient Case — A Step-by-Step Guide

This walkthrough is designed for your first live consultation using VitalMatrix. It assumes you have completed the orientation session.

---

## Before the consultation

**Choose the right first case:**
For your first case, choose a patient with a complex, multi-system presentation — ideally someone whose case you already understand well. This lets you compare the VitalMatrix output against your existing clinical picture and give meaningful feedback on accuracy.

Avoid starting with a single-system presentation. The terrain engine's value is most visible in multi-node cases.

**Prepare the data you will enter:**
- Primary presenting symptoms (3–6 is ideal for a first case)
- Available lab results (even partial results are useful)
- Key terrain history flags (stress load, sleep, dietary pattern, significant prior events)

---

## During the consultation

You do not need to use VitalMatrix in real time during the consultation if you prefer not to. Many practitioners find it more natural to complete data entry immediately after the session from their consultation notes.

If you do use it in real time, the recommended approach is:
1. Run your consultation as normal
2. Enter data into VitalMatrix in the final 5–10 minutes
3. Review the output with the patient's case in mind — do not share the screen with the patient

---

## After the consultation — step by step

**Step 1:** Enter presenting symptoms. Be specific. "Fatigue" is less useful than "persistent fatigue, worse in the afternoon, present for 8 months, not relieved by sleep."

**Step 2:** Enter available lab data. If using functional reference ranges, flag them in the input.

**Step 3:** Enter terrain history flags. Even approximate information (e.g. "high stress load for past 2 years") improves node weighting.

**Step 4:** Generate the Working Terrain Architecture.

**Step 5:** Review the Node Burden Map. Ask yourself: does the node distribution match my clinical impression? If not, note the discrepancy — this is valuable feedback.

**Step 6:** Review the Cascade Pathway Indicator. Does it identify cascade activity you would have expected? Does it surface anything you might have deprioritised?

**Step 7:** Use the Protocol Scaffold as your starting structure. Annotate it with your clinical adjustments.

**Step 8:** Save or export the document.

## After your first case — send feedback

Email hello@vitalmatrix.co.uk with:
- A brief summary of the case type (no patient-identifiable information)
- Whether the Node Burden Map reflected your clinical assessment (yes / partially / no)
- Any nodes that were over- or under-weighted in your view
- Any protocol scaffold items that were not clinically relevant for this case type

This feedback is the most valuable thing you can give the platform in Phase 1. It is read by Dr Faisal's team and feeds directly into terrain engine calibration.

## Common questions after the first session

**"The node burden distribution didn't match my clinical picture."**
This is expected in early cases. Enter the case data and your clinical assessment via email. It will be reviewed.

**"The Protocol Scaffold included items I would not use for this patient."**
Remove them. The scaffold is a starting structure, not a prescription. Your clinical judgement supersedes it.

**"I'm not sure how to interpret the FLINT™ score."**
The FLINT™ score is a composite terrain load indicator. A higher score indicates greater overall terrain burden across the node architecture. It is most useful for tracking change over time — not for a single-session snapshot. Compare across sessions with the same patient.

**"Can I share the output with my patient?"**
Not directly, and not in its current format. The Working Terrain Architecture is a practitioner document. You may explain its contents to your patient in clinical language, but do not share it as a written report. A patient-facing summary format is planned for Phase 2.
