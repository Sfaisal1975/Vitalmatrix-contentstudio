## About Section (2,600 characters max — aim for 1,800–2,000 for readability)

I have practised functional medicine for 26 years.

For most of that time, I built IFM matrices by hand. Session by session. Patient by patient. I mapped terrain signals across biological systems, identified cascade interactions, weighted node burden, and produced clinical documentation that reflected how terrain medicine actually works — manually, for every complex case.

The clinical methodology was right. The infrastructure was not there.

Functional medicine demands systems thinking at a level that conventional clinical software was never designed to support. The tools built for general practice record diagnoses. They don't compute terrain relationships.

So I built what should have existed.

**VitalMatrix** is a clinical intelligence platform for functional medicine practitioners. It maps presenting symptoms, laboratory findings, and terrain history across the NCZ™ architecture — seven biological nodes, five terrain zones, 5,000+ terrain computation algorithms — and generates a working terrain architecture document for practitioner review.

The document is what I used to spend hours producing. The platform produces it in under 60 seconds. The clinical decision remains with the practitioner — that is by design, and by regulatory requirement.

VitalMatrix is not a diagnostic system. It is not a patient-facing tool. It is infrastructure for the way functional medicine actually works.

**Phase 1 is open now** — 10 founding practitioner slots, for registered functional medicine practitioners in the UK who want to shape how the platform develops. If that sounds like you, my calendar is open.
