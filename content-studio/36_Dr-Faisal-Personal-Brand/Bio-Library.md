## Long Bio (≈ 400 words)

> Dr Shahzad Faisal MBBS, FAAMFM has practised functional medicine for 26 years. His clinical career spans NHS practice, private practice including Harley Street, and a long-standing commitment to terrain-based medicine as the most coherent framework for addressing complex, chronic, multi-system presentations.
>
> Dr Faisal holds fellowship of the American Academy of Functional Medicine (FAAMFM) — one of the highest professional designations in the field internationally. His clinical approach is grounded in IFM terrain methodology: mapping biological burden across interconnected systems rather than pursuing isolated diagnoses, and designing interventions that address root terrain conditions rather than symptom suppression.
>
> After two decades of building IFM matrices by hand — patient by patient, session by session — Dr Faisal recognised that the infrastructure needed to practise terrain medicine at scale did not exist. The tools available to functional medicine practitioners were either designed for conventional diagnostics or were generic health technology not built for the structural demands of terrain-based clinical documentation.
>
> In 2026 he founded VitalMatrix to address that gap. VitalMatrix is a clinical intelligence platform that processes presenting symptoms, laboratory findings, and terrain history through the NCZ™ architecture — a proprietary framework mapping seven biological nodes across five terrain zones — and generates a working terrain architecture document for practitioner review. The platform computes terrain relationships at a depth and speed that reflects the complexity of real functional medicine practice, without removing clinical judgement from the process.
>
> VitalMatrix is not a diagnostic system. It is infrastructure. The terrain architecture it generates is a structured starting point — the document that used to take hours to produce, produced in seconds, ready for the practitioner's clinical reasoning.
>
> Dr Faisal leads the founding phase of VitalMatrix personally, working directly with the initial cohort of ten founding practitioners whose clinical feedback shapes the terrain engine's development. He is based in the United Kingdom.
